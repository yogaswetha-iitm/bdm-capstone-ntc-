Problem & Diagnosis

The core challenge was identifying workflow inefficiencies hindering production targets. Our diagnosis involved a multi-faceted approach, from process mapping to root cause analysis, to uncover the underlying issues affecting cycle times, defect rates, and resource utilization.

Pareto Analysis of Defects

The Pareto principle (80/20 rule) was applied to pinpoint the most significant defect categories. This analysis revealed that a few key areas were responsible for the majority of production rework and delays, allowing for focused improvement efforts.

Root Cause Analysis

A Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram was used to explore the root causes of workflow disruptions. This structured analysis helped categorize potential issues and trace them back to fundamental problems in methods, machinery, materials, and manpower.

→
Method: Lack of standardized procedures and inconsistent process adherence.
→
Machine: Absence of automation (CNC machines) and frequent breakdowns of older equipment.
→
Manpower: Skill gaps in critical areas like welding, and fatigue issues on night shifts.
→
Material: Inconsistent quality and delays from primary suppliers, leading to high rejection rates.

Solutions & Impact

Based on the analysis, a series of short-term and long-term recommendations are proposed. These data-driven solutions aim to address the identified root causes, streamline the workflow, and create a more resilient and efficient production environment.

Short-Term Actions (Immediate Fixes)

  • ✓
    Strengthen Defect Control: Implement real-time defect tracking at critical stages (fitting, welding, painting) and enhance operator training to minimize errors.
  • ✓
    Optimize Maintenance: Create a structured preventive maintenance schedule for high-breakdown machines (204, 206, 207) to reduce unexpected failures.
  • ✓
    Address Shift Gaps: Adjust scheduling to balance workloads between shifts and implement structured rest breaks for night shift workers to mitigate fatigue.

Long-Term Strategies (Sustainable Improvements)

  • ✓
    Invest in Automation: Introduce CNC machines for cutting and marking to improve precision, reduce manual effort, and increase throughput.
  • ✓
    Diversify Supplier Base: Strengthen supplier evaluation criteria and explore alternative sourcing to reduce dependency and mitigate risks from underperforming vendors.
  • ✓
    Data-Driven Decision System: Implement real-time production monitoring dashboards and use predictive analytics to forecast machine failures and prevent unplanned downtime.
